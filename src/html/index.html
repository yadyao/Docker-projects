Introduction

My name is Marie Yao and I always dreamed of traveling the world and getting my degree while experiencing another culture. My favorite subject in school was science, and it was a lightbulb moment when my chemistry teacher conducted a presentation on petroleum chemical products, oil extraction and production. It was with no hesitation that I joined petroleum engineering undergraduate program at Texas Tech after accepting an international scholarship along with Prize of the Inter-African Conference of Majors as part of the Excellence Banquet of the Essor group (one of my biggest achievements to date). Upon getting my petroleum bachelors degree, I joined the workforce as Fracking Field Engineer and became one of the supervisors of my frack team. This leadership role helped me learn to listen to and address major concerns, manage difficult situations and empower my team members. The Covid 19 pandemic slow down gave me the best opportunities to further my education. I joined a Devops Engineer course and I started working at Landmark Technologies for almost 4 years.
